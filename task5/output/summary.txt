**Macroeconomic and Industry Risks**

* Global and regional economic conditions can affect demand for the company's products and services.
* Adverse economic conditions can lead to financial instability, insolvency, and reduced liquidity among suppliers and contract manufacturers.
* Uncertainty or decline in global or regional economic conditions can impact consumer confidence and spending.

**Political and International Risks**

* Political events, trade disputes, war, terrorism, and natural disasters can harm or disrupt international commerce and the global economy.
* Restrictions on international trade, such as tariffs and export controls, can materially affect the company's operations and supply chain.
* Global climate change can lead to more frequent and intense natural disasters, affecting the company's supply chain and operations.

**Business Interruptions**

* The company's operations and facilities are subject to the risk of interruption by fires, power shortages, industrial accidents, terrorist attacks, and other events beyond the company's control.
* A business interruption can require substantial recovery time, significant expenditures to resume operations, and loss of sales.

**Competition and Technological Change**

* The company operates in highly competitive global markets with rapid technological change.
* The company may be unable to compete effectively in these markets if it fails to introduce innovative new products, services, and technologies.
* Competitors may infringe on the company's intellectual property, and effective protection is not consistently available in every country where the company operates.

**Insurance and Other Risks**

* The company's insurance coverage may be insufficient to cover all losses that may arise from business interruptions, natural disasters, and other events.
* The company's ability to develop and market new products and services successfully is uncertain.

Overall, the company faces various risks that could impact its business, including macroeconomic and industry risks, political and international risks, business interruptions, competition and technological change, and insurance and other risks.
